Papers

I'm currently an author on 2 papers:

  • On Automatic Parsing of Log Records for the ICSE NIER 2021 Conference, and;
  • Serial memory error patterns in bilinguals and monolinguals from the Proceedings of Meetings on Acoustics

For the ICSE paper, I worked with Professor Andriy Miranskyy to create some Recurrent Neural Network models using Keras and TensorFlow to analyze Apache log strings.

For the paper for the Journal of Language and Memory, I created both the PRE Algorithm and the Rational TE Algorithm to analyze transpositional errors in number recall.

To learn more about these papers, reach out to me!

I'm grateful to have been able to co-found Coterie: Collective Memories Inc., where I worked as the company's initial CTO.

I built the initial version of the application using a React + Redux frontend and an Express backend, entirely in TypeScript.

I helped with the company setup, and located the designer who provided Coterie with its fantastic logo.

I'm incredibly proud of the company's mission and origin, and the fact that we paired with sustainable print providers to provide an eco-friendly product.

In August 2021, I parted ways with Coterie to focus on my responsibilities at Top Hat, but I continue to excitedly watch the company's growth and am thrilled about the product's launch.

Labs

I built and wrote a shader lab for Toronto Metropolitan University's Virtual Reality course, in which students learn the fundamentals of Unity's shaders and experiment with simple shader examples.

The lab has been available to students starting in the Winter 2021 semester at TMU.

I've been a part of two fantastic clubs at TMU: the Association of Ryerson Role-players and Gamers (ARRG!) and the TMU AI team.

With ARRG!, I helped create the ARRG D&D group, which coordinates regular Dungeons& Dragons games for TMU students and pairs players up with Game Masters. I later became the Vice-President and President of the club, where we managed to recruit over 100 new players.

With the TMU AI team, I worked with the Engineering department to create a TMU AI mobile app, which is hopefully coming soon!
